Carpentry is hard work

Posted 8 June 2022

I figured I had found the perfect hobby: carpentry. I figured I could kill two birds with one stone. I would learn a very useful trade as a hobby, but also I could build some pieces of furniture for my own home which could potentially save money. I thought I had arrived at the holy grail of pastimes.

I decided to study all sorts of furniture plans, studied the necessary tools and placed a budget. I figured I would start building tables, chairs, shelves, etc. I acquired all the necessary tools: circular saw, jig saw, miter saw, table saw, chisels, hand saw, lots of lumber. The one tool I wish I had are planers.

Anybody's first carpentry project has to be a workbench. I built two of them. I wanted to use these work benches as ‘infrastructure’ to facilitate my other pieces of furniture. I was very excited.

I ended up building a few tables, a kitchen island, a patio picnic table with a bench. One thing I realized, carpentry is not a hobby, it is REAL.HARD.WORK. It is very physical and a very involved craft. Carpenters are hard workers.

This hobby turned out to be so much hard work that I eventually evolved into a learning type of carpentry job like baseboarding or crown molding and door framing. I still got all my tools and I know how to do new things.
