Audible is awesome

Posted 8 June 2022

I am putting together the list of books I want to keep reading by the end of the year. I have been interested in monetary policy lately and I have been reading a few books on the topic. I also want to catch up on books regarding the future of tech. Finally I am also interested in topics related to personal improvement and spiritual growth.

I have been consuming my books in Audible by Amazon, it is very comfortable and it allows me to listen at night before bed. I usually put on my audible book before bed for a couple of hours which helps me disconnect from the noise of a regular work day. Highly recommended. However, books are about 20 dlls each and so if you keep buying like me it can get expensive. However, there are some books that are free. I picked up Wealth of Nations free of charge, which is a title I meant to read for some time.

Stuff I bought :) is below. I have a few more books on my wish list.

  • Atomic Habits.
  • The wealth of nations.
  • The mystery of banking.
  • The ascent of money.
  • An Economic History of the World since 1400.
  • The Coming of Neo-Feudalism: A Warning to the Global Middle Class.
  • Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order.
  • Range: Why Generalists Triumph in a Specialized World.
  • Our Final Invention: Artificial Intelligence and the End of the Human Era.
  • The Innovator's Dilemma: When New Technologies Cause Great Firms to Fail.
  • Lying.
  • Human Compatible: Artificial Intelligence and the Problem of Control.
  • Extraordinary Popular Delusions and the Madness of Crowds and Confusion de Confusiones.
  • Longitude.
  • The Sovereign Individual: Mastering the Transition to the Information Age.

I need more books on personal growth so I am still browsing around. I have already read about 3 other books on monetary policy not listed so I think I am going to give it a rest on that for a while because it’s getting repetitive.
