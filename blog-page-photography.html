Photography can be fascinating

Posted 7 June 2022

Right before I graduated with my PhD, my wife and I both came up with the idea to build a foody webpage. It was supposed to be a vegetarian food blog we would name ‘vegetables with sauces.’

We figured, if we were to create a successful foody web page, I would need to learn how to take a good photo for that blog. We quickly bought a camera and accessories for $500 dlls we found on Craigslist. It was a Cannon EOS Rebel T100. Back then money was very tight so this was considered a gigantic investment for us. I was so excited to learn this complex but cool discipline.

I wanted to really understand the process of what it’s required to take a good photo, what is really behind it. The good news for contemporaneous learners is: we are some of the luckiest people alive. We have the power of the internet: youtube is an endless source of learning if you use it for the right purpose.

I spent tons of time studying photography and ended up learning how to use Photoshop and Lightroom. I loved Lightroom, everyone should learn how to use it. It is not difficult and it adds a lot of style into your photos-- it makes a big difference.

Something I quickly discovered in the basics of photography: there are a few configurations that are essential in taking the photo that you want. They call it the ‘Exposure Triangle,’ when I heard this term that was it for me… I got hooked into learning photography. Whenever any discipline has a mythical name like the “Exposure Triangle” then I am easy prey. Whenever you have a Triangle in a discipline it usually means you need to find a balance between them to create your desired product. It exists in the blockchain too, except there they call it a Blockchain Trilemma.

The exposure triangle is a relation between shutter speed, aperture and ISO, and knowing the right configuration of these three parameters is essential to create your desired product. If you change any one of those settings you will need to compensate on the other two in order to produce a feasible photo. Not everyone enjoys learning these details and so nowadays, cameras can determine these settings automatically. However any photographer who wants to produce unique images you need to understand how these settings work. The automatic setting will not always produce that shot you want to deliver. Consider checking my Panning Exercises post on my small photography blog I created a few years ago, using the camera auto settings would make that exercise impossible.

I have stopped posting photos, life gets busy and there are other very interesting things to learn about. I am looking into buying an iphone 13 which supposedly has the ability to take photos in RAW format -- essential in photography-- maybe in a few months.
