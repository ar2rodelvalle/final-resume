Video gaming during the Pandemic

Posted 12 September 2016

I very much enjoy video gaming. I have bought the latest games and have finished a few of them. I don’t always play, but whenever I find a good game I usually like to play it all the way. My favorite games in the current console are Demon Souls, and Horizon Forbiden West (currently playing it).

During the pandemic, I felt playing Call of Duty with my cousins was a very good alternative to stay in touch. We still do that once a week. Call of Duty has had issues with cheating, where players modify the source files in the game to get an unfair advantage over the competition. Activision is trying to fix this issue using a solution called Ricochet, which has been modestly succesful. I don't track this issue very closely, to be honest.

Currently, I am in the process of finishing Horizon Forbidden West on PS5, but this time I am playing it slowly, as I don’t have much time to focus on it. I spend about 1-2 hours of play time per week. I bought Elder Ring as well, but I hear it's a game similar to Demon Souls, which takes a good amount of tries to advance levels. I don't see myself playing this game anytime soon.

I feel video games take off the stress and worries of adult life for me. I will keep coming back to the next games, which just keep getting better and better.
