Video gaming during the Pandemic

Posted 12 September 2016

I very much enjoy video gaming until today. I have bought the latest video games and finished a few of them. I don’t always play but when I find a good game I usually like to play it all the way. My favorite games in the current console are Demon Souls, and Horizon Zero (currently playing it) Also I found that during the pandemic, playing Call of Duty with my cousins was a very good alternative to stay in touch. We still do that every once in a while. Call of Duty has had an issue with cheating where players modify the source files in the game, which gives them an unfair advantage over the rest of the competition.

Currently, I am in the process of finishing the Horizon Zero game but this time I am playing it slowly, as I don’t have much time to focus on it. I spend about 1-2 hours of play time per week. I bought Elder Ring as well, but I understand it is a game similar to Demon Souls, which takes a good amount of tries to advance levels.

I find that video gaming really takes my stress and worries about adult life away so I will keep coming back.
